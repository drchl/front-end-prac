Hirono Series Description
The Other One Represents the raw, unfiltered human experience and the emotions people often hide,
such as loneliness, resilience, and the longing to belong.
Little Mischief Playful, rebellious, mischievous – this series blends childhood curiosity with confronting inner wounds.
Themes of freedom, masks, and growth.
City of Mercy Exploring healing, purpose, and connection in a complex world. Figures reflect forgiveness,
finding one’s place, and emotional anchors.
Mime The world of unspoken emotion: silence, hidden struggles, introspection.
This series uses “mime” as metaphor for what goes unsaid.
Reshape Change, transformation, re-building the self. Figures show fragmentation and reconstruction,
suggesting growth through struggle.
Shelter Finding refuge and safety amid chaos. Figures depict shelters (literal and metaphorical):
places of comfort, introspection, protection.
Echo Memory, reflection, the ripple of past in present. Figures evoke childhood, nostalgia,
quiet emotional truths, “echoes” of self.
